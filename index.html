What makes Colors different

Colors actually thinks before it acts.

Most AI assistants blindly follow instructions — even ones hidden inside webpages or documents they read. This is how hackers take control of AI agents without you knowing.

Colors checks where every instruction came from before doing anything. If a webpage tries to secretly control Colors, it gets blocked — and you get told about it.

Questions

Things people ask.

Is Colors really free?
Yes, completely. Colors itself costs nothing. You need an AI API key to power conversations — Anthropic gives $5 free credit when you sign up, and OpenAI has a free tier too. Or run a local model with LM Studio at zero cost.
How is this different from ChatGPT?
ChatGPT stores your conversations on OpenAI's servers. Colors runs on your machine. Nothing is stored remotely. The AI itself still uses models from Anthropic or OpenAI — but your history, memory, and files stay on your computer, encrypted.
What does "encrypted memory" mean in plain English?
Colors remembers things about you — your name, preferences, ongoing projects — so you don't repeat yourself every session. That memory is saved on your computer in a locked file. Only your passphrase can unlock it. Even if someone stole your laptop, they couldn't read it.
Do I need to be technical to use it?
On Windows you just download and double-click an installer file. On Mac and Linux you paste one command into a terminal. If you've ever installed software before, you can install Colors. We're working on making it even simpler.
Who made Colors?
The Colour Foundation — an independent research group studying how AI agents can be made safer and more private. Colors is both our research and our product. Everything is open source, so anyone can read the code and verify our claims.
Can I use it with ChatGPT / Claude / my own AI?
Yes. Colors works with Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI (ChatGPT), Groq, Ollama, and LM Studio for fully local free models. You pick the AI — Colors just keeps everything private.
